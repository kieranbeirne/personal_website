Who am I?

My name is Kieran Beirne and I am a computer science student at Texas A&M, graduating in May 2024. I am graduating with an emphasis in psychology which I hope to use for creating accessible and inclusive software. I have interests in software engineering and DevOps and I am currently pursuing a software engineering internship for Summer 2023. I graduated from Southlake Carroll High in 2020, where I was a varsity cross country runner, which culminated in a state championship my senior year. My hobbies and interets include running, coffee, skydiving and movies. In college, I have taken leadership roles for two clubs, Memorial Student Center Freshmen in Service and Hosting (MSC FISH) and the Student Engineers' Council (SEC).



Memorial Student Center Freshmen in Service and Hosting

MSC FISH is a service organization that provides leadership opportunities aimed at enhancing its members' purpose and identity by engaging in community service and program development on campus and within the community. I served as the Leadership and Diversity Director of MSC FISH from 2021-2022. I curated and presented internal workshops over topics such as implicit bias, privilege and culture to all 90 members of MSC FISH. I mentored 8 freshmen over the course of the year and we all still keep in touch today. Being in MSC FISH was a fantastic way to give back to the community and develop close friendships in college. I highly encourage all A&M students to get involved in Freshman Leadership Organizations (FLOs).



Student Engineers' Council

SEC is the representative voice of all engineering students at Texas A&M. I currently serve as the Systems Administrator which means updating and improving SEC's 14 websites. I lead a 12-person committee and manage projects with a focus on agile development. With this position I have been able to learn and utilize AWS, Ruby on Rails, ASP.NET, MongoDB, React, Javascript and countless other technologies and frameworks. In Winter 2021 I was selected to represent the SEC on our annual ProTrip in Boston Massachusetts. Some of the websites I develop and maintain are sec.tamu.edu, eweek.tamu.edu, envision.sec.tamu.edu, legislation.sec.tamu.edu, engrseminars.tamu.edu, and careerfair.sec.tamu.edu.
